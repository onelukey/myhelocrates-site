Best HELOC Lenders in 2026: Top Picks Compared

May 2026 10 min read MyHelocRates Team

The best HELOC lender isn't always the one with the lowest rate — it's the one that fits your specific situation: your credit score, how much you want to borrow, how you plan to draw funds, and whether you value an online-only experience or a local branch relationship.

We've reviewed the most widely available HELOC lenders in the US market for 2026, comparing them on the factors that actually matter: rates, fees, draw flexibility, minimum credit requirements, maximum CLTV, and real customer experience.

Important: Rates Change Daily

HELOC rates are variable and tied to the Prime Rate. The ranges shown reflect typical advertised starting rates as of mid-2026 and will vary based on your credit score, LTV, and lender. Always get a personalised quote directly from the lender.

What to Look For Beyond the Rate

  • Draw period length — most are 10 years; shorter periods limit your flexibility
  • Repayment period — typically 20 years; longer means lower monthly payments after draws end
  • Annual fees — some lenders waive in year one but charge $50–$500 annually after
  • Inactivity fees — charged if you don't draw a minimum amount within a set period
  • Early closure fees — some lenders charge if you close within 2–3 years
  • Rate cap — the maximum your rate can ever reach; critical protection in a rising rate environment
  • Minimum draw amounts — some lenders require $10,000–$25,000 at closing
Always Get at Least Three Quotes

HELOC rates and terms vary enormously between lenders — even on the same day. Getting quotes from your existing bank, a credit union, and an online lender gives you real leverage and ensures you're not leaving money on the table.
